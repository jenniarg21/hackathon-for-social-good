Feeling out of sorts? You are not alone.

Consider the impact that the pandemic has had on us all.

Very early on, the National Center for Health Statistics began to capture the more personal effects of increased stress and isolation on the national population. A slight upward trend in use of mental health care can be seen here, and it’s no surprise. If you’ve felt the weight of the world grow heavier, you’re not alone.

Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. (2022). Mental Health Care in the Last 4 Weeks. Retrieved June 27, 2022 from https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/mental-health-care-in-the-last-4-weeks

Learn More

This line graph represents the percentage of people in the U.S. over 18 that 1) received counseling or therapy, 2) needed counseling or therapy but did not get it, 3) took prescription medication for mental health, or 4) took prescription medication for mental health and/or received counseling or therapy. Data was gathered at different points in time from August 2020 through May 2022, and responses referred to the previous four weeks.

The data was collected through the Household Pulse Survey, conducted by the National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS) which partnered with the Census Bureau to rapidly respond and provide relevant information about the impact of the coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic in the U.S.

More information about the survey is provided here.

If you or someone you love is overwhelmed by stress, find resources near you with the National Alliance on Mental Illness local chapter.
